Top 10 Cruise Drink Package Tips

Learn how to maximize your drink package value and avoid common mistakes that cost you money.

Planning your next cruise and wondering if a drink package is worth it? You're not alone! Many cruisers struggle with this decision, and it can make or break your vacation budget. After analyzing hundreds of cruise experiences, we've compiled the top 10 tips to help you make the smartest choice.

1. Calculate Your Daily Consumption

Before booking any drink package, honestly assess your drinking habits. Most cruise lines require you to purchase the package for the entire cruise duration, so there's no "trying it out" for a day. Use our drink package calculator to see exactly where you stand.

2. Don't Forget About Non-Alcoholic Drinks

Many cruisers focus only on alcoholic beverages, but premium coffee, fresh-squeezed juices, and specialty teas can add up quickly. A latte here and a smoothie there can easily reach $20+ per day. Most drink packages include these beverages, making them much more valuable than you might think.

3. Consider Port Days vs Sea Days

On port days, you'll likely be off the ship exploring, which means fewer opportunities to use your drink package. If your cruise has many port days, you might be better off paying as you go. However, if you have several sea days, a package becomes much more attractive.

4. Watch for Pre-Cruise Discounts

Cruise lines often offer significant discounts (20-40%) when you purchase drink packages before your cruise. These deals are usually available through your cruise line's website or travel agent. Never pay full price for a drink package!

5. Understand Package Limits

Most drink packages have price limits per drink. For example, a "Classic" package might cover drinks up to $10, while "Premium" covers drinks up to $19. If you prefer premium spirits or top-shelf wines, you'll pay the difference. Know your package's limits before ordering.

6. Share with Caution

Drink packages are strictly non-transferable and cruise lines actively monitor for sharing. Getting caught can result in package cancellation without refund. It's simply not worth the risk.

7. Factor in Gratuities

Remember that drink packages don't include gratuities. You'll typically pay 18-20% on top of the package price. Our calculator includes this in the total cost, so you get the true picture.

8. Consider Your Travel Companions

Many cruise lines require all adults in the same cabin to purchase drink packages if one person does. This can significantly impact your decision, especially if your travel companion doesn't drink much.

9. Don't Overlook the Convenience Factor

Beyond the financial calculation, consider the convenience. With a package, you can try new drinks without worrying about the cost. You can order premium coffee without guilt. This "freedom" can enhance your vacation experience.

10. Use Our Calculator for Real Numbers

The most important tip: don't guess! Use our interactive drink package calculator to see exactly how much you'll spend with and without a package. It accounts for your specific cruise length, discount rates, and drinking preferences.

Pro Tip

Most cruisers who drink 4+ alcoholic beverages per day (or 6+ total drinks including coffee/juice) will save money with a drink package. The key is honest assessment of your consumption habits.

Final Thoughts

Drink packages can be an excellent value, but only if they match your consumption patterns. Take the time to calculate your actual costs, consider the convenience factor, and don't forget about pre-cruise discounts. With the right approach, you can enjoy your cruise without worrying about your bar tab!
