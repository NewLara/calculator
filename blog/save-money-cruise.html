How to Save Money on Your Next Cruise

Budget-friendly strategies to make your cruise vacation more affordable without sacrificing fun.

Cruising is one of the best values in travel, but costs can add up quickly if you're not careful. From drink packages to excursions, there are many ways to overspend on a cruise. Here are our proven strategies to save money while still having an amazing vacation.

1. Book During Wave Season (January-March)

Why it works: Cruise lines offer their best deals during the first quarter of the year. You can save 20-40% on your cruise fare, plus get additional perks like drink packages, onboard credit, or free gratuities.

Pro tip: Set up price alerts on cruise booking sites and be ready to book quickly when deals appear.

2. Choose Your Cabin Wisely

Interior vs. Balcony: Interior cabins can save you $500-1000+ per person compared to balcony cabins. While balconies are nice, you'll spend most of your time exploring the ship and ports anyway.

Location matters: Avoid cabins near elevators, nightclubs, or below the pool deck to ensure a good night's sleep without paying extra.

3. Use Our Drink Package Calculator

Don't guess about drink packages! Use our interactive calculator to see exactly if a drink package saves you money. Many cruisers overestimate their consumption and end up losing money on packages.

4. Book Excursions Independently

Ship excursions: Convenient but expensive (often 30-50% more than booking directly)

Independent tours: Research companies with good reviews on TripAdvisor or Cruise Critic. Many offer the same experiences for much less.

Tech tip: Use apps like Viator or GetYourGuide to compare prices and read reviews before booking.

5. Take Advantage of Free Activities

Modern cruise ships offer tons of free entertainment:

  • Broadway-style shows
  • Comedy clubs
  • Live music
  • Trivia games
  • Dance classes
  • Poolside activities

6. Eat at Included Restaurants

Most cruise ships have excellent included dining options. Save specialty restaurants for special occasions or use onboard credit to pay for them.

Pro tip: Many ships offer lunch at specialty restaurants for half the dinner price.

7. Bring Your Own Wine

Most cruise lines allow you to bring 1-2 bottles of wine per person. Pay the corkage fee ($15-25) and still save money compared to buying wine onboard.

8. Use Onboard Credit Strategically

If you receive onboard credit as a booking perk, use it for:

  • Specialty dining
  • Spa treatments
  • Excursions
  • Drink packages (if it makes sense)

9. Book Future Cruises Onboard

Cruise lines offer significant discounts and onboard credit when you book your next cruise while still on the ship. Even if you're not sure about your next vacation, you can often transfer the booking later.

10. Use Credit Card Rewards

Many travel credit cards offer cruise-specific benefits:

  • Statement credits for travel purchases
  • Priority boarding
  • Onboard credit
  • Free checked bags

11. Pack Smart to Avoid Extra Fees

Bring essentials: Sunscreen, medications, and toiletries are expensive onboard

Formal night: Pack appropriate attire to avoid buying expensive clothing

Electronics: Bring your own camera instead of buying photos

12. Use Free Internet When in Port

Cruise ship internet is expensive and often slow. Instead:

  • Use free WiFi at port cafes and restaurants
  • Download maps and content before your cruise
  • Consider a local SIM card for longer stays

13. Consider Repositioning Cruises

These one-way cruises (when ships move between regions) often offer incredible value. You can get 10-14 day cruises for the price of 7-day ones.

14. Book Last Minute (If Flexible)

If you can travel on short notice, last-minute deals can save you 30-50%. Cruise lines would rather sail with a full ship at a discount than empty cabins.

15. Join Cruise Line Loyalty Programs

Even if you've only cruised once, sign up for loyalty programs. Benefits often include:

  • Onboard credit
  • Free internet
  • Priority boarding
  • Exclusive events

Final Thoughts

The key to saving money on cruises is planning ahead and being strategic about your spending. Use our calculator to make informed decisions about drink packages, research excursions before booking, and take advantage of all the free activities your ship offers.

Remember, the goal isn't to deprive yourself of fun - it's to spend your money on experiences that matter most to you. Whether that's a special excursion, a spa treatment, or premium dining, make sure your spending aligns with your priorities.
